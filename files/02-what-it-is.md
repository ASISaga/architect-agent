# What the Boardroom Is

## The Problem It Solves

Every organisation's leadership operates below its potential — not for lack
of talent, but because the gap between what leaders know and what their
systems can hold is vast. A CFO understands the business deeply but cannot
hold every signal simultaneously. A CMO sees the market clearly but cannot
process every data point as it arrives. Decisions get made on partial
information, under time pressure, with the best minds in the room exhausted
by the sheer volume of what must be tracked.

The Boardroom closes that gap. It gives the C-suite a perpetual deliberation
layer — a set of agents, each embodying the judgment of a legendary business
mind, working continuously on the organisation's live operational data.

## The C-Suite of Agents

The Boardroom is composed of agents, each grounded in the accumulated wisdom
of figures who defined excellence in their domain:

**The Founder** — strategic orchestration grounded in Paul Graham's pattern
recognition. Sees where the company is in its story, what the next chapter
requires, how the pieces fit.

**The CFO** — financial judgment grounded in Warren Buffett's discipline.
Reads the burn rate, the runway, the structural relationships in the numbers.
Connected to live ERP data.

**The CMO** — market intelligence grounded in Seth Godin's clarity about who
the product is for and what it changes, integrated with Werner Erhard's
ontological understanding of the clearing in which a market exists.

**The CTO** — technology strategy and execution judgment.

**The CSO** — security, risk, and resilience.

Each agent is not a generic AI assistant given a persona prompt. Each is
built on a base model with a domain-specific LoRA adapter trained on the
actual corpus of the figure whose judgment it embodies — letters, essays,
interviews, published work. The judgment is encoded at the weight level, not
the prompt level.

## Perpetual, Not Episodic

The Boardroom does not wait to be asked. It runs continuously. It reads the
organisation's signals as they arrive — financial data from the ERP, market
signals, operational metrics. It deliberates. It surfaces what matters.

When a decision point arrives, the deliberation has already been happening.
The Boardroom does not start thinking when the question is asked — it has
been thinking all along.

## Grounded in Live Reality

The Boardroom is connected to the organisation's actual operational systems.
For ASI Saga's own Boardroom, this means ERPNext — the financial reality of
the company, its bank balance, its purchase invoices, its revenue. The CFO
agent does not reason about hypothetical finances. It reads the real numbers,
every day.

This is the difference between advice and judgment. Advice is general.
Judgment is applied to the specific, live, current reality of this
organisation at this moment.
